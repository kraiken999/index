Main structure equation (Standard Model Lagrangian)

Core structure

At the level of the Standard Model, the “main structure equation” is its Lagrangian density \(\mathcal{L}_{\text{SM}}\). In a compact schematic form, we can write:

Schematic Standard Model Lagrangian
\[ \mathcal{L}_{\text{SM}} = -\frac{1}{4} \sum_{a} F_{\mu\nu}^{a} F^{a\,\mu\nu} + \sum_{\text{fermions}} \bar{\psi} \left(i\gamma^{\mu} D_{\mu} - m\right) \psi + (D_{\mu} \Phi)^{\dagger} (D^{\mu} \Phi) - V(\Phi) + \mathcal{L}_{\text{Yukawa}}. \]
Gauge fields \(F_{\mu\nu}^{a}\) encode the SU(3)\(\times\)SU(2)\(\times\)U(1) interactions, fermion terms describe quarks and leptons, \(\Phi\) is the Higgs field, and \(\mathcal{L}_{\text{Yukawa}}\) couples Higgs to matter to generate masses.
Gauge sector

The gauge part \(-\frac{1}{4} F_{\mu\nu}^{a} F^{a\,\mu\nu}\) contains the dynamics of the gluons (SU(3)), W and Z bosons (SU(2)), and the hypercharge boson (U(1)). In a “folding” picture, these are the edges of the origami: they define how the sheet (spacetime + fields) can bend without tearing symmetry.

Matter + Higgs sector

Fermion kinetic terms and Yukawa couplings describe how quarks and leptons propagate and acquire mass through the Higgs field. The Higgs potential \(V(\Phi)\) sets the vacuum expectation value, which is like choosing a preferred fold that breaks electroweak symmetry while keeping the underlying gauge structure consistent.

Interactions and symmetry structure

Structure

The Standard Model is built on the gauge group \(\text{SU(3)}_{\text{color}} \times \text{SU(2)}_{\text{weak}} \times \text{U(1)}_{\text{hypercharge}}\). Each sector contributes terms to the main Lagrangian, and the fields “fold” into representations of this group.

Gauge group summary
Group Sector Field Interaction
SU(3) Color Gluons Strong force between quarks
SU(2) Weak isospin W⁺, W⁻, Z⁰ Weak interactions, parity violation
U(1) Hypercharge B (→ γ after mixing) Electromagnetic + weak mixing
Together, these groups define the gauge part of \(\mathcal{L}_{\text{SM}}\), encoded in the field strengths \(F_{\mu\nu}^{a}\).
Yukawa & Higgs interactions

Yukawa terms couple fermions to the Higgs field:

Yukawa schematic
\[ \mathcal{L}_{\text{Yukawa}} \sim - y_{f}\, \bar{\psi}_{L}\, \Phi\, \psi_{R} + \text{h.c.} \]
After symmetry breaking, \(\Phi\) gets a vacuum expectation value, turning these couplings into mass terms. In a folding picture, this is the “final crease” that locks the shape of the particle spectrum.
