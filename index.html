Universal Quantum Origami equation

The universal equation collects all allowed fold operations—mountain, valley, hinge motion, and tube closure—into a single operator-based framework. It is the backbone that connects the “thisone(1)” paper to particle physics and cosmology.

Schematic form (conceptual)
\(\mathcal{U} = \sum_{i} F_i(\theta_i, \phi_i, \kappa_i, t)\, \mathcal{O}_i\)  with  \(\mathcal{O}_i \in \{\text{mountain}, \text{valley}, \text{hinge}, \text{tube}, \dots\}\)  // each particle and structure is a discrete pattern of active operators \(\mathcal{O}_i\) and parameters \((\theta_i, \phi_i, \kappa_i, t)\)

In the “thisone(1)” paper, the basic fold operators \(\mathcal{O}_i\) are defined on a 2D sheet. The universal equation extends this by allowing base points to move, turning hinges into tubes and closed loops that represent circular particles.

Foundations

The “thisone(1)” paper establishes the geometric rules for consistent folding: mountain/valley assignments, hinge constraints, and compatibility conditions. These rules become the axioms of the universal equation.

When we restrict \(\mathcal{U}\) to pure 2D folds with fixed base points, we recover the original framework. Allowing base points to move along trajectories extends the same rules into tubes and circular particles, without changing the underlying equation—only the active operator set and parameter ranges.
