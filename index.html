Main Structure Equation (Quantum Origami Core Structure)

Core Structure

In Quantum Origami, the fundamental “main structure equation” is the global configuration of all folded energy sheets. Each sheet \(P_i \subset \mathbb{R}^2\) carries a finite crease pattern and signed fold angles. It is rigidly folded by a map

\[ F_i : P_i \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3, \]

and placed in space by a rigid motion

\[ T_i \in SE(3). \]

The complete universe at a given moment is the union of all folded and placed modules:

\[ \boxed{ C = \bigcup_{i=1}^{N} T_i\!\left(F_i(P_i)\right) } \]

This is the standardized configuration equation appearing in the Quantum Origami papers and defines the entire geometry of the universe as a single origami assembly.
